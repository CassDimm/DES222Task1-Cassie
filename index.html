Being in place
Relationships with spaces
Over 960 million tourists travelled internationally in 2022. How do they connect to new places? How do they understand where they are?
PAST
1914: Technological advancement in transportation allowed passengers to experience a ride in the sky.


1955: More people in the US travelled by air than by train. Air travel was now a necessity. It was the way to connect the world.


2001: Air travel changed forever, with security measures being implemented worldwide. The advancement of technology meant a more streamlined process was put in place.
2007: The creation of iPhone and the rise of social media created ways for people to connect to the places they visited and share their adventures with friends and family back home. Pictures or it didn't happen.
PRESENT
Whilst augmented and virtual reality in tourism had existed prior to 2020, Covid saw it grow rapidly as a way to help connect the world. Now people can experience cities in new ways, connecting through their phones. They don't even have to leave their homes.
With 91% of the world owning a smart phone, apps have become a way to connect with places. From directions, to live language translation, to gamification of surroundings, there are so many ways to engage with places in unique ways.
FUTURE
Will digital connection destroy human connection?
